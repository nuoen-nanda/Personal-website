Path to learning programming

Thanks to technology advancement, there are unlimited programming resources I can search for on the internet. At the beginning, I have very little idea about programming. Therefore, I completed the Havard CS50 course to grab the basic idea of computer science and various kinds of languages including Scratch, C++, Java and Python, etc. The instructors illustrate the concept well. If you are new to programming, I recommended you to complete this course. After having a rough idea what programming is, it is the time to know the most popular languages at the moment. In the meantime, I saw an article in medium introducing different platforms to learn programming lanague. There is one called CodeGym. It's a platform to learn Java. I was attracted by its teaching way. Wwe need to solve the tasks and level up ourselves. Thus I chose to learn Java finally. Together with Java: A Beginner's Guide, I have learnt the basic syatax including array, loops, objects and if statement, etc.


After finishing ten levels of CodeGym course, I did not feel very right about Java and hesitated whether I should continue the study and consider to switch to a new language. Then I happened to come across more information about Javascript. I realized it together with HTML and CSS are very powerful languages to develop website. I thought I could make use of my marketing experiences to become a front-end programmer. Since then, I subscribed the membership of Codecademy as it offers a systematic course for becoming a front-end programmer. Currently I am learning Git and Github, I believe that by learning it can enable me to share and learn with other programmer more efficiently and effectively. And here this is my first proejct to apply what I have learnt so far!
